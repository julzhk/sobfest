Announcing The First UK Recovery Film Festival

September 2020, International Recovery Month, sees the launch of the first ever UK Recovery Film Festival in London. This inaugural British Cultural Event will be a celebration of recovery from addiction, mental ill health and trauma through film. Our first year will champion young people and aims to address the mental health crisis facing this vulnerable population.

Held over three days in a stylish independent cinema, it will kick off with a launch party on Friday, followed by our gala screening. The next two days, will be jam-packed with the an eclectic and exciting mix of cutting edge features, shorts, and documentariess from around the world - most of which will not have been shown in Britain.

The highlight of the festival will be the screening of the winners in our UK youth filmmaker's competition for ages 16-26. These original and unique films will raise awareness of and experiences of addiction and associated disorders in their community. Along with these new insightful films we will put in some old classics with a spin.

Addiction, metal ill health and trauma are interlinked, and can begin at a young age and follow a chronic path. We seek to arrest the development of these health issues by early education and awareness arising. Highlighting that recovery is possible by showing films that demonstrate this and working with people who are all in recovery and thriving. We want young people from around the country to get involved and have their say about these issues which are effecting them, their friends, family and community.

After the films we will be holding Q&A’s with the young winners, professional filmmaker’s, directors and addiction, mental ill health and trauma luminaries from around Britain. And a few celebs for good measure.

Join us to celebrate at this unique, youth orientated, cultural event. That aims to show that recovery is possible through film and bring together the community around the country who are seeking help, want to learn more, work in the sector and want to be part of the movement towards creating social change and destigmatisation.

Young Filmmaker's Competition

We will be asking for original film and documentary submissions on the themes of recovery, addiction, mental ill health and trauma. Prioritising entries from young people, age of 16-26. They can be first time filmmakers without experience, emerging talent, students, it open to all. We actively encourage applicants from those with disabilities, BAME, LGBT and other diverse groups. We are looking for shorts, features and animations. These can be shot on any format including smart phone. We will be posting a short film on the website explaining how to make a film with a mobile phone. The films will be judged by their content and ideas rather than technical brilliance to ensure inclusivity.

Call For Worldwide Submissions

We will also be asking for UK and International submissions from filmmakers over the age of 26 which educate, inform and promote well being and understanding of our themes.

The UK Recovery Film Festival will receive submissions through the Film Freeway Portal. A donation of £10 per entry for filmmakers over the age of 26/and or professionals will be put towards the festival costs.

A panel of judges from the world of film and media, who have personal experience of our themes and who are in recovery, will be appointed to assess the entries.

Sponsorship and Donors

We are looking for sponsors who want to become involved in this event, who have a passion for creating change and highlighting the crucial need for young people’s addiction and mental health treatment, as well as the wider population.

Donors may wish to contribute by sponsoring a film. Or by sharing expertise or time to support the event. Could you help us reach every part of the UK and its young people through your marketing and social media? Could you provide regional support to help young people make their films?

Ticket sponsorship

We want to provide free tickets for young people aged 16-26, and to offer travel bursaries, could your organisation donate funds towards this goal? Maybe your organisation could offer a funded place on a film training scheme for our young competition winners?
